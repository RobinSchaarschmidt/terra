# Terra Applied Scenario — Language and Understanding Asymmetry

## Scenario Overview

A planetary governance proposal affects multiple countries, communities, and institutions.

The proposal is submitted in English, but affected participants speak different languages and operate with different levels of technical, legal, and scientific understanding.

The issue is not only translation.

The issue is unequal access to meaning.

---

## Core Problem

Language asymmetry can create governance inequality.

A participant may be formally included but practically excluded if they cannot understand:

- the proposal
- the risk
- the evidence
- the consequences
- the decision process

Formal participation without semantic access is invalid participation.

---

## Affected Layers

This scenario affects:

- governance
- validation
- interfaces
- trust scoring
- Guardian Layer
- constitutional rights

Step 3 — Semantic Translation Requirement

The system does not allow simple word-for-word translation only.

It requires:

* literal translation
* contextual translation
* simplified explanation
* technical explanation
* local-impact explanation

Each version must preserve meaning.

⸻

Step 4 — Understanding Level Mapping

Affected participants are mapped by access level:

* expert
* administrative
* community representative
* general public
* low-literacy / non-technical audience

Each group receives a different explanation depth.

The content changes in complexity, not in meaning.

⸻

Step 5 — AXIOMA Semantic Consistency Check

AXIOMA compares all language versions.

It checks:

* meaning preservation
* missing risk terms
* altered obligations
* softened consequences
* misleading simplifications

If meaning diverges:SEMANTIC_CONFLICT

The proposal cannot proceed.

⸻

Step 6 — Local Context Validation

Local validators review whether translated explanations match:

* local legal context
* cultural meaning
* practical consequences
* regional risk perception

The system does not erase local meaning because it is inconvenient.

⸻

Step 12 — Final Validation

Proposal may proceed only if:

* semantic consistency is validated
* affected groups had meaningful access
* unresolved translation risks are documented
* local objections are preserved

Possible result:VALIDATED_WITH_SEMANTIC_CONDITIONS
Step 13 — Execution Constraints

If approved, execution requires:

* ongoing multilingual reporting
* local-language monitoring updates
* accessible dispute channels
* re-translation if terms change

⸻

Failure Conditions

The scenario fails if:

* a proposal proceeds in one dominant language only
* affected groups cannot understand consequences
* simplified explanations hide risk
* local meanings are dismissed
* consent is counted without comprehension

⸻

System Response Summary

Terra responds by:

* detecting language asymmetry
* requiring semantic translation
* validating meaning across languages
* mapping understanding levels
* delaying decisions until access is real
* preserving local interpretations

⸻

Objective

Ensure that planetary governance does not become domination by language, education level, or technical vocabulary.

Participation is valid only when meaning is accessible.
