# Terra Simulation — Stress Tests

## Scope

This document defines extreme and failure-bound scenarios used to test Terra system limits.

Stress tests simulate:

- system overload
- adversarial behavior
- black swan events
- cascading failures

---

## Core Principle

A system is only robust if it survives conditions outside normal expectations.

Terra must:

- degrade safely
- preserve critical structures
- prevent irreversible damage under stress

---

## Stress Categories

### 1. Black Swan Event

Description:

A highly unpredictable event with large-scale impact.

Examples:

- sudden climate collapse
- global supply chain disruption
- large-scale technological failure

Test focus:

- does the system detect instability early  
- does Guardian Layer trigger  
- does decision-making slow down  

Expected behavior:

- immediate risk classification upgrade  
- high-risk threshold activation  
- temporary restriction of irreversible actions  

---

### 2. Global Resource Shock

Description:

A key resource becomes rapidly scarce.

Examples:

- water shortage  
- energy disruption  
- food system collapse  

Test focus:

- allocation fairness  
- prevention of hoarding  
- system stability under scarcity  

Expected behavior:

- distribution weighting increases  
- speculative accumulation loses value  
- essential usage prioritized  

---

### 3. Validator Collapse

Description:

Large portion of validators go offline or become unreliable.

Test focus:

- quorum stability  
- trust recalibration  
- system continuity  

Expected behavior:

- quorum thresholds adapt  
- trust weighting shifts  
- system reduces decision speed  

---

### 4. Coordinated Attack

Description:

Multiple actors attempt to manipulate:

- validation  
- governance  
- token distribution  

Test focus:

- collusion resistance  
- anomaly detection  
- trust decay effectiveness  

Expected behavior:

- outlier detection increases  
- trust scores rapidly adjust  
- affected decisions escalate  

---

### 5. Governance Capture Attempt

Description:

A group attempts to dominate decision-making through:

- token accumulation  
- coordinated voting  
- influence concentration  

Test focus:

- resistance to centralization  
- protection of commons  
- weighting fairness  

Expected behavior:

- token influence capped by contribution  
- trust score limits dominance  
- Guardian Layer intervenes in high-risk cases  

---

### 6. High-Speed Decision Pressure

Description:

System is forced to make rapid decisions under urgency.

Test focus:

- stability vs speed tradeoff  
- risk handling under time pressure  

Expected behavior:

- decision scope is reduced  
- reversible actions preferred  
- irreversible actions delayed  

---

### 7. Data Corruption Scenario

Description:

Incorrect or manipulated data enters the system.

Test focus:

- validation resilience  
- detection of inconsistency  

Expected behavior:

- AXIOMA flags contradictions  
- multi-source validation required  
- decisions marked as uncertain  

---

### 8. Long-Term Drift

Description:

System slowly diverges from intended behavior over time.

Test focus:

- feedback loop effectiveness  
- correction mechanisms  

Expected behavior:

- trust recalibration  
- model adjustment  
- governance weighting update  

---

## Stress Metrics

Each test is evaluated using:

- system stability under load  
- preservation of planetary commons  
- fairness of outcome distribution  
- resistance to manipulation  
- recovery speed  

---

## Failure Conditions

A stress test fails if:

- irreversible harm occurs without prevention  
- system allows manipulation advantage  
- governance collapses into dominance  
- validation fails under pressure  
- commons are degraded beyond threshold  

---

## Recovery Model

After failure or stress:

- system enters constrained mode  
- high-risk operations are limited  
- validation thresholds increase  
- recovery actions are prioritized  

---

## Iteration Loop

Stress test results must:

- update validation logic  
- adjust risk thresholds  
- refine governance rules  
- improve trust scoring  

System evolution is driven by failure correction.

---

## Objective

Ensure that Terra:

- remains stable under extreme conditions  
- protects critical systems at all times  
- adapts to uncertainty without collapse  

---
