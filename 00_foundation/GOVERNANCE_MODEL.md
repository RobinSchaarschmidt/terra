# Terra — Governance Model

## Scope

This document defines how Terra governs decisions at planetary scale.

It explains:

- how decisions are made  
- how power is constrained  
- how trust is established  
- how long-term stability is enforced  

Terra governance is not based on authority.  
It is based on validated impact.

---

## Core Principle

No entity governs alone.

No decision is valid unless it satisfies:

- evidence  
- system compatibility  
- long-term stability  

---

## Governance Structure

Terra governance is composed of five interacting layers:

```text
Constitution
→ Economics
→ Governance
→ Validation (AXIOMA)
→ Guardian Layer


Each layer constrains the next.

⸻

1. Constitution

Defines:

* non-negotiable limits
* planetary commons
* irreversible boundaries

Function:

No governance decision may violate constitutional constraints.

⸻

2. Economics

Defines:

* value creation
* resource allocation
* responsibility mapping

Function:

Aligns incentives with real-world impact.

⸻

3. Governance Layer

Defines:

* decision processes
* participation rules
* conflict resolution

Function:

Transforms proposals into structured decisions.

⸻

4. Validation Layer (AXIOMA)

Defines:

* truth approximation
* consistency checks
* distributed verification

Function:

Prevents decisions based on incomplete or manipulated information.

⸻

5. Guardian Layer

Defines:

* long-term risk protection
* delay mechanisms
* proof enforcement

Function:

Prevents irreversible damage and systemic instability.

⸻

Decision Process

Every decision follows:Proposal
→ Validation
→ Quorum
→ Governance
→ Guardian Check
→ Execution
→ Monitoring

Participation Model

Participation is not equal by default.

Influence is determined by:

* relevance to the decision
* contribution history
* trust score
* validated impact

This prevents:

* uninformed dominance
* passive influence accumulation
* manipulation through volume

⸻

Token Integration

Terra uses a multi-token system:

* TERRA → long-term alignment
* GOVT → active participation
* CREDIT → value accounting
* WORK → activity tracking

Tokens influence governance but do not control it.

⸻

Decision Criteria

A valid decision must:

* be supported by evidence
* pass validation
* respect constitutional limits
* remain reversible if uncertain
* minimize long-term harm

⸻

Risk Handling

Decisions are classified:

* Low Risk
* Medium Risk
* High Risk
* Planetary Risk

Higher risk increases:

* validation depth
* quorum requirements
* Guardian involvement

⸻

Conflict Resolution

Conflicts are resolved through:

* evidence comparison
* impact analysis
* long-term prioritization

If unresolved:

→ default = minimal irreversible harm

⸻

Minority Protection

Minority positions are preserved if they:

* identify unaddressed risk
* provide valid evidence
* expose hidden impact

Majority agreement does not eliminate valid dissent.

⸻

Language and Access

Governance requires:

* semantic translation
* accessible explanations
* local interpretation validation

Participation is invalid without understanding.

⸻

Transparency

All decisions must be:

* traceable
* auditable
* explainable

No hidden processes are allowed.

Trust Model

Trust is:

* dynamic
* earned
* continuously updated

Based on:

* validation accuracy
* consistency
* outcome alignment

Trust influences weight, not authority.

⸻

Failure Handling

If governance fails:

* decisions are delayed
* high-risk actions are restricted
* validation thresholds increase
* Guardian Layer intervenes

Failure triggers system correction.

⸻

Evolution

The governance model evolves through:

* simulation
* stress testing
* real-world feedback

Changes require:

* strong validation
* demonstrated improvement
* system-wide consistency

⸻

Comparison to Existing Systems

Traditional Governance:

* authority-based
* majority-driven
* short-term oriented

Terra Governance:

* validation-based
* impact-driven
* long-term constrained

⸻

Objective

Ensure that:

* decisions reflect reality
* power cannot be concentrated
* long-term stability is preserved
* all participants have meaningful access

⸻

Final Statement

Terra does not aim to replace governance.

It aims to make governance accountable to reality.
