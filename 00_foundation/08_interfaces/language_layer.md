# Terra Interfaces — Language Layer

## Scope

This document defines how Terra handles language, meaning, and understanding across different participants.

Language is not treated as a translation problem.  
It is treated as a system access problem.

---

## Core Principle

Participation without understanding is invalid.

A participant must be able to:

- understand the proposal  
- understand the risks  
- understand the consequences  
- understand their role  

Otherwise, their participation cannot be counted as valid input.

---

## Language vs Meaning

Terra separates:

- language (words)  
- meaning (semantics)  

Translation must preserve meaning, not just wording.

---

## Language Detection

For every proposal, Terra detects:

- original language  
- affected regions  
- required target languages  
- local communication constraints  

---

## Translation Layers

Each proposal must be available in multiple forms:

### 1. Literal Translation

Direct translation of the original content.

---

### 2. Contextual Translation

Adjusted to reflect:

- legal context  
- cultural understanding  
- regional interpretation  

---

### 3. Simplified Version

Explained in:

- non-technical language  
- accessible structure  
- reduced complexity  

---

### 4. Technical Version

Full detail for:

- experts  
- validators  
- auditors  

---

### 5. Local Impact Version

Focused on:

- what changes locally  
- who is affected  
- what risks exist  

---

## Semantic Validation

AXIOMA must validate that:

- meaning is consistent across languages  
- risks are not softened or removed  
- obligations remain unchanged  
- no ambiguity is introduced  

If inconsistency is detected:

```text
SEMANTIC_CONFLICT
The proposal cannot proceed.

⸻

Understanding Levels

Participants are mapped into categories:

* expert
* administrative
* community representative
* general public
* low-literacy / non-technical

Each group receives different explanation depth.

Meaning remains constant.
Complexity is adjusted.

⸻

Comprehension Requirement

Before participation is counted, Terra requires evidence of access:

* translated material delivered
* explanation sessions or equivalents
* feedback or questions recorded
* acknowledgment of understanding

No comprehension → no valid participation

⸻

Interface Requirements

Interfaces must:

* allow language switching
* show meaning equivalence
* highlight differences if present
* provide explanation layers

⸻

Minority Interpretation

Local interpretations must be preserved if they:

* identify additional risk
* reflect cultural or practical differences
* expose hidden consequences

The system stores multiple valid interpretations.

⸻

Governance Interaction

Governance must consider:

* semantic consistency
* access equality
* interpretation differences

A decision cannot proceed if:

* affected groups lack understanding
* translation is incomplete
* meaning diverges across languages

⸻

Guardian Interaction

The Guardian Layer activates if:

* semantic access is insufficient
* language asymmetry creates power imbalance
* critical risks are not properly communicated

⸻

Continuous Translation

Translation is not static.

If a proposal evolves:

* all language versions must update
* semantic validation must re-run

⸻

Failure Conditions

The system fails if:

* one language dominates decision-making
* translation hides risk
* complexity blocks participation
* meaning is distorted across regions

⸻

Objective

Ensure that:

* language does not create hidden power
* meaning remains consistent across participants
* all affected actors have real access to decisions
