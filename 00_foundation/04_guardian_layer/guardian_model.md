# Terra Guardian Layer — Model

## Scope

The Guardian Layer is a non-political system responsible for protecting long-term planetary stability.

It does not govern.  
It does not initiate policy.  

It enforces:
- validation depth  
- risk awareness  
- system integrity  

---

## Core Principle

No system may override long-term stability for short-term gain.

The Guardian Layer ensures that this principle is enforced across all decision layers.

---

## Function

The Guardian Layer operates as a constraint mechanism.

It can:
- delay decisions  
- require additional proof  
- escalate validation requirements  
- trigger system-wide review  

It cannot:
- create policy  
- override validated consensus without justification  
- act arbitrarily  

---

## Trigger Conditions

Guardian activation occurs when:

- planetary commons are at risk  
- irreversible actions are proposed  
- impact exceeds defined thresholds  
- uncertainty is too high  
- validation is incomplete  

---

## Proof Demand System

The Guardian Layer can issue a Proof Demand.

This requires:

- higher-quality data  
- expanded modeling  
- independent validation  

If proof demand is not satisfied:

→ decision cannot proceed

---

## Risk Classification

All decisions are classified:

- Low Risk → minimal Guardian interaction  
- Medium Risk → partial validation enforcement  
- High Risk → full Guardian intervention  

High Risk includes:

- irreversible environmental impact  
- systemic instability  
- cross-generational consequences  

---

## Delay Protocol

Delay is a core function.

When triggered:

- execution is paused  
- additional validation is required  
- system simulation may be enforced  

Delay protects against premature decisions.

---

## Multi-Layer Validation

The Guardian Layer does not act alone.

It integrates with:

- AXIOMA (consistency validation)  
- distributed validators (JAM)  
- domain-specific expertise  

No single entity controls the Guardian process.

---

## Neutrality Constraint

The Guardian Layer must remain:

- non-political  
- non-economic  
- non-aligned  

It evaluates systems, not actors.

---

## Escalation Logic

If risk exceeds critical thresholds:

- decision is halted  
- broader system involvement is triggered  
- planetary layer review is required  

---

## Override Conditions

A Guardian intervention can only be overridden if:

- stronger evidence is provided  
- risk is demonstrably reduced  
- validation thresholds are exceeded  

Override without proof is invalid.

---

## Transparency Requirement

All Guardian actions must be:

- publicly traceable  
- logically explainable  
- verifiable  

No hidden interventions are permitted.

---

## Failure Mode

If the Guardian Layer fails:

- system defaults to minimal irreversible action  
- high-risk operations are restricted  
- decision scope is reduced  

Safety overrides speed.

---

## Temporal Authority

The Guardian Layer operates across time.

It accounts for:

- delayed effects  
- cumulative damage  
- system feedback loops  

---

## System Objective

Ensure that:

- no irreversible harm is accepted without proof  
- all critical decisions are validated beyond majority opinion  
- long-term stability is preserved  

---

End of Guardian Model
