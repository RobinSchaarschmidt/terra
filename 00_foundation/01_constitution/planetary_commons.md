# Terra Constitution — Planetary Commons

## Scope

This document defines the structure, classification, and usage constraints of planetary commons.

Planetary commons are systems that:
- sustain life  
- operate across borders  
- cannot be isolated without systemic damage  

They exist outside traditional ownership models.

---

## Definition

A planetary common is any system that:

- is essential for long-term human survival  
- operates as an interconnected global process  
- cannot be replaced once degraded beyond threshold  

Commons are not assets.  
They are shared dependencies.

---

## Core Domains

### 1. Atmosphere

Includes:
- air composition  
- greenhouse gas balance  
- temperature regulation  

Constraints:
- no ownership  
- emissions require accounting and balancing  
- irreversible atmospheric damage is prohibited  

---

### 2. Water Systems

Includes:
- oceans  
- rivers  
- groundwater cycles  
- hydrological balance  

Constraints:
- no privatization of core cycles  
- usage must not disrupt regeneration  
- contamination requires full accountability  

---

### 3. Biodiversity

Includes:
- species diversity  
- ecosystems  
- genetic resources  

Constraints:
- extinction-level impact is prohibited  
- ecosystem collapse triggers intervention  
- extraction must not exceed regeneration capacity  

---

### 4. Climate Systems

Includes:
- global temperature dynamics  
- feedback loops  
- large-scale environmental interactions  

Constraints:
- destabilizing actions require proof of safety  
- system-level risks override local decisions  

---

### 5. Human Foundation Layer

Includes:
- basic survival conditions  
- physical safety  
- access to essential resources  

Constraints:
- no system may deny minimum survival conditions  
- large-scale harm to populations is invalid  

---

## Usage Rights

Commons cannot be owned.

Only usage rights may be granted under conditions:

- defined scope  
- measurable impact  
- time limitation  
- revocability  

Usage rights are:

- conditional  
- auditable  
- non-permanent  

---

## Responsibility Mapping

Every usage action must define:

- who uses  
- what is used  
- impact magnitude  
- duration  
- recovery plan  

Unmapped impact = invalid action

---

## Regeneration Requirement

Any use of a common must include:

- regeneration model  
or  
- compensation mechanism  

Net-negative impact without recovery is prohibited.

---

## Threshold System

Each domain operates with thresholds:

- safe zone  
- stress zone  
- critical zone  

Crossing into critical zones triggers:

- automatic review  
- restriction or halt of activities  
- system-wide notification  

---

## Measurement Standard

All commons require:

- continuous monitoring  
- open data access  
- verifiable metrics  

No decision is valid without measurable reference.

---

## Conflict Handling

If usage conflicts arise:

priority is determined by:

1. system survival  
2. long-term stability  
3. human necessity  
4. economic benefit  

Economic interest alone is insufficient justification.

---

## Enforcement Logic

Violations of commons rules result in:

- restriction of usage rights  
- increased validation requirements  
- economic penalties (via Terra systems)  

Severe violations trigger system-level intervention.

---

## Temporal Responsibility

Responsibility does not end with action.

Actors remain accountable for:

- delayed effects  
- indirect damage  
- system interactions  

Time-delayed harm is treated as direct harm.

---

## Objective

Ensure that all planetary commons:

- remain stable  
- remain accessible  
- remain functional across generations  

---

End of Planetary Commons
