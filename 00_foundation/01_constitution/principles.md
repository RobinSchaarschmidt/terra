# Terra Constitution — Principles

## Scope

Terra defines a constitutional layer for planetary-scale coordination.  
It constrains systems where their actions affect shared global resources.

---

## Foundational Premise

The Earth is a shared system under stewardship.  
It cannot be treated as an asset.

---

## Planetary Commons

The following domains are non-transferable:

- Atmosphere  
- Water systems  
- Biodiversity  
- Climate systems  
- Fundamental human dignity  

Only conditional usage rights are allowed.

---

## Rights and Limits

### Rights

Every human has the right to:
- Exist within a stable planetary system  
- Access essential resources  
- Participate in long-term-impact systems  

### Limits

No action is valid if it:
- Causes irreversible system damage  
- Externalizes cost to future generations  
- Centralizes control over commons without justification  

---

## Stewardship

All actors are stewards.

This implies:
- Responsibility over time  
- Preservation of system integrity  
- Accountability beyond immediate outcomes  

---

## Time Dimension

All decisions must be evaluated across:

- Short-term  
- Mid-term  
- Long-term  

Decisions without long-term modeling are incomplete.

---

## Proof Requirement

Claims must be supported by:

- Data  
- Models  
- Distributed validation  

---

## Reversibility

Uncertain decisions must remain reversible.

Irreversible actions require higher validation thresholds.

---

## Transparency

All relevant actions must be:

- Traceable  
- Auditable  
- Interpretable  

---

## Conflict Resolution

Resolved through:

- Evidence  
- System impact  
- Multi-source validation  

Default fallback:
Minimal harm + preservation of commons

---

## Evolution

Changes require:

- Proven necessity  
- Broad validation  
- Demonstrated long-term improvement  

---

## Objective

Ensure:

- Planetary stability  
- Fair responsibility distribution  
- Alignment with physical reality
