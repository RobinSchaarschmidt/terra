# Terra Governance — Decision Model

## Scope

This document defines how decisions are proposed, evaluated, validated, and resolved within Terra.

Governance is not based on authority.  
It is based on structured validation of impact.

---

## Core Principle

No decision is valid by majority alone.

A valid decision must satisfy:
- evidence  
- system compatibility  
- long-term stability  

---

## Decision Structure

Every proposal must define:

- objective  
- affected systems  
- resource usage  
- expected outcomes  
- risk profile  
- time horizon  

Incomplete proposals are invalid.

---

## Decision Layers

Decisions operate across three layers:

### 1. Local Layer
- limited scope  
- immediate impact  
- low system risk  

### 2. Regional / System Layer
- cross-boundary effects  
- shared resource usage  
- medium risk  

### 3. Planetary Layer
- affects commons  
- long-term consequences  
- high systemic risk  

Higher layers override lower layers when conflict exists.

---

## Validation Requirements

Each decision must pass:

### Evidence Check
- data support  
- measurable assumptions  

### Impact Check
- effect on planetary commons  
- externalities identified  

### Time Check
- short-term vs long-term balance  

### Risk Check
- reversibility  
- failure scenarios  

Failure in any category blocks progression.

---

## Participation Model

Participation is determined by:

- relevance to affected system  
- proven contribution history  
- trust score (from validation systems)  

Not all participants have equal weight.

---

## Weighting Mechanism

Influence is derived from:

- TERRA (long-term alignment)  
- GOVT (active participation)  
- trust score (validation reliability)  

Pure token ownership is insufficient.

---

## Conflict Resolution

When disagreement occurs:

Resolution follows:

1. Evidence comparison  
2. Impact minimization  
3. Long-term stability priority  
4. Multi-source validation  

If conflict persists:

→ system defaults to lowest irreversible harm

---

## Proof Demand Mechanism

Any participant may trigger a proof demand.

This requires:

- stronger evidence  
- deeper validation  
- broader consensus  

Unmet proof demand = decision delay or rejection

---

## Reversibility Rule

If uncertainty is high:

- decision scope is reduced  
or  
- temporary implementation is enforced  

Irreversible actions require maximum validation.

---

## Delay Mechanism

High-risk decisions are automatically delayed.

Delay is not failure.  
It is a system safety function.

---

## Minority Protection

Minority positions are preserved if:

- they identify unaddressed risk  
- they provide valid evidence  

Consensus does not eliminate valid dissent.

---

## Execution Constraint

A decision is only executed if:

- validation thresholds are met  
- no critical system risk remains  
- impact is measurable  

---

## Feedback Loop

All decisions enter post-analysis:

- outcome vs prediction  
- error measurement  
- trust score adjustment  

This updates future decision weighting.

---

## Failure Handling

If a decision causes harm:

- responsible actors are identified  
- corrective action is enforced  
- system parameters are adjusted  

Failure improves system accuracy.

---

## Governance Objective

Ensure that:

- decisions reflect reality  
- risk is minimized  
- long-term stability dominates  

---

End of Decision Model
